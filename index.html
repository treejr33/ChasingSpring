Introduction1 / 8

Context: The Rhythm of Life

  • Chasing Isotherms: Migratory birds don't just fly north; they follow the 5°C - 10°C isotherm (the "Green Wave") for optimal resources.
  • Energy Balance: Migration is a precise calculation of energy. Birds must refuel at specific Wetland Stopovers.
  • The Problem: What happens if these stopovers are destroyed? Habitat loss creates an "Energy Crisis" that breaks the migration chain.

Research Background2 / 8

Research Question & Objectives

"How does habitat availability and energy consumption determine the survival of migratory birds?"

1. Visualize

Map 3 major flyways (East, Mid, West) with real-time tracking logic tracking the 5°C line.

2. Analyze

Provide a "Data Cockpit" to compare flight distance, elevation profile, and latitude.

3. Gamify

Implement a "Survival Mode" where users can simulate wetland destruction.

Data & Methodology

DATA SOURCES

Biological Data: Simulated monthly coordinates based on scientific tracking records of Red-crowned Cranes and Bar-headed Geese.

TECH STACK

Leaflet.js: Base map rendering & TileLayers.

Key Findings & Results

Isotherm Dependency

Visual analysis confirms birds move north strictly within the 5-10°C zone. They track the "Green Wave" of spring resources.

The "West" Challenge

Bar-headed Geese show extreme elevation gain crossing the Himalayas (8000m+). This requires maximum energy efficiency.

Survival Mode

Simulating the destruction of a key node (e.g., Yellow River Delta) leads to Energy Depletion (0%) before arrival.
